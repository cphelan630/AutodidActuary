Book Reviews

Books that stuck with me

My Reading List

Thinking, Fast and Slow

By Khaneman and Tversky

I first read this around 2017. I was fresh out of high school, where a lot of my essays were about paradigm shifts and metacognitive awareness. This book (1) provided a framework to think more about this theme of epistemic humility, (2) really caused me to reflect on how "experience" is just a more developed, tried and true System 1, (3) showed me that people aren't statistically intuitive, which pushed me further down the actuarial path, and finally (4) inspired me to try to emulate the intellectual appraoch to life taken by the authors.

I really liked Veritasium's views on experts just having more a developed system 1. Veritasium lecture which is similar to the message in Malcom Gladwell's Outliers. That whole 10,000 hours is a demonstration of the veracity of Occam's Razor. Simply put in the time and effort and convert your system 2 understanding to system 1 instincts.

Projections

By Karl Diesseroth

Andrew Huberman introduced me to this guy, who also has a fascinating conversation with Lex Fridman. He pioneered the field of optogenetics, which is a technique that uses light to control neurons within living tissue. This book is a deep dive into the science of the brain and how it shapes our thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. Diesseroth is an insanely good writer, and I can see how he built up his system 1 to be proficient with some of his deliberate practices such as ending every day forcing himself to think in full sentences. I still struggle to focus on my breathe when meditating, so I consider him my sensei in this regard.

Three Body Trilogy

Cixu Liu

I guess I like science fiction now. These books are a great way to escape and drift off into thought of what's possible. So detailed and easy to visualize. Simultaneously causes you to think momento mori and also holy shit with compounding effects who knows what the accumulation of knowledge is going to lead to. Netflix butchers this book, don't even bother.
